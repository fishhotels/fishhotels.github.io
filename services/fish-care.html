Fish Care & Management

Proper care ensures the well-being of aquatic life inside our AquaDomes. Learn how to responsibly maintain clean, safe, and sustainable habitats.

✔ Basic Care Requirements

  • Maintain clean, filtered water at all times.
  • Perform regular partial water changes (20–25% weekly).
  • Feed fish appropriate, nutrient-rich food in small portions.
  • Monitor lighting and ensure natural day-night cycles.
  • Keep temperature within species-safe ranges.
Tip: Overfeeding is the #1 cause of tank pollution — feed what fish can finish in 1–2 minutes.

✔ Water Quality Management

  • Test pH, ammonia, nitrate, and nitrite levels weekly.
  • Use dechlorinated water to avoid harming fish.
  • Ensure strong biological filtration within the dome.
  • Remove debris or uneaten food promptly.

✔ Health Monitoring

  • Observe fish daily for changes in movement or colour.
  • Check for common signs of stress like hiding or rapid breathing.
  • Quarantine sick fish when needed to prevent spread.
  • Keep records of feeding, cleaning, and behaviour changes.

✔ Long-Term Management Practices

  • Rotate cleaning schedules for equipment inside the dome.
  • Track fish growth and provide habitat upgrades as needed.
  • Promote biodiversity by introducing compatible species only.
  • Educate staff and partners on sustainable aquatic welfare.

✔ Environmental Responsibility

  • Dispose of water responsibly to avoid re-pollution.
  • Use eco-safe cleaning products.
  • Support conservation programs to protect wild habitats.
Our mission: **Preserving aquatic life through sustainable, pollution-free solutions.**
